Work Experience

Since I originally helped out my Father out on the road in his 18 Wheeler at the age of 11, I have a wealth of experience in the workforce, from a number of different industries.

Food and Beverage

I began my time in the Food and Beverage industry at the age of 16, working as a part-time dishwasher at a Mom and Pop Italian restaurant. After 2 years there, I desired to expand my skills beyond the dish pit, and managed to gain employment for White Spot Restaurants in Kamloops, BC, as a line cook. At White Spot, I was thoroughly trained in the safe and proper preparation of menu items. Still desiring to continue honing my craft, I accepted a chef d'parti position for the Sun Peaks Resort Corporation in Sun Peaks, BC. I thoroughly enjoyed my time at Sun Peaks, where I had numerous memorable experiences, such as planning and cooking the menu at a private party for former Olympian and Gold Medalist, Nancy Greene. I would work in many more restaraunts after my time at Sun Peaks came to an end, before transitioning to the Hospitality Indurstry.

Hospitality

I spent many years working in the hospitality industry as a Hotel Concierge, most notably at The Lord Nelson Hotel in Halifax, Nova Scotia. Being a CN rail heritage hotel, my appreciation for history was quite useful as I was able to give tours to hotel guests regarding the background of the Historic Property. As a concierge, my extensive formal research of the Province allowed me to be an exceptional source of knowledge for any guest who was looking to experience Nova Scotia. I served many high profile guests, including former United States Ambassador Robert Mandell, and 2019 NBA Champion Toronto Raptor Danny Green. This taught me a great deal about professionalism, in regards to proper dress, etiquette, and communication.

Teaching Experience

During my Bachelor of Education, I completed 20 weeks worth of teaching experience in the form of practicum. My first ten weeks completed at J.L. Ilsley High School in Spryfield, Nova Scotia, teaching Canadian History 11 and Global History 12 under Mr. David Harris. It was at J.L. Ilsley where I found my passion for Senior High teaching. I took responsibility for the majority of the day to day planning and management. The following year, I completed my second 10 weeks of practicum at St. Andrew's Junior School in Antigonish, Nova Scotia, under the supervision of Ms. Shari MacGillivray. My time at St. Andrews Junior School thoroughly prepared me for the intensity of Middle School teaching, as well as providing me with the experience in teaching sewing and cooking classes in a safe and constructive manner. I thoroughly enjoyed and cherished my time at both my host schools, and am thrilled to be entering into this career path.
