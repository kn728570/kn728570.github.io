Education

Undergraduate

I began my Post-Secondary education in 2015, starting a Bachelor of Arts Degree, majoring in Psychology. After my first two years, I realized that while I enjoyed studying Psychology on my own time, the heavily Neuroscience based program at Dalhousie was not suited for me. I had enjoyed my supplementary Political Science courses up until this point, and decided to pursue it as a major, despite being rather uninitiated to the discipline. It was in Political Science that I realized I love the study of all things related to society, and how individuals and groups behave within it. As a result, I have supplemented by Political Science credits with courses in History, Anthropology, Early Modern Studies, and more.

In addition to my study of humanity and society, I enrolled in courses that provided some level of practical experience. For example, I took a course on Electronic Information Management, which was essentially a course on Microsoft Word, PowerPoint, Excel, and basic HTML5 and CSS coding (the basis for this website). As another example, I took a course on basic human anatomy, which has been quite handy in practical situations.

Bachelor of Education

I completed my Bachelor of Education at St. Francis Xavier University, Class of 2022. The program was quite rigorous, thoroughly preparing me for a career as an educator. At St. F.X., I learned the importance of community, positivity, and inclusiveness. I completed my first practicum at J.L. Ilsley High School in Spryfield, Nova Scotia, teaching Canadian History 11 and Global History 12 under Mr. David Harris.
